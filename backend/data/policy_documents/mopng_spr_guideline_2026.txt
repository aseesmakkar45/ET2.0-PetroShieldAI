Ministry of Petroleum & Natural Gas (MoPNG)
Government of India
Subject: Emergency Drawdown and Operational Management of Strategic Petroleum Reserves (SPR)
Circular No: MoPNG/SPR-2026/04
Date: June 11, 2026

NEW DELHI — Under the powers conferred by the National Energy Security Act, the Ministry of Petroleum & Natural Gas, in consultation with the Indian Strategic Petroleum Reserves Limited (ISPRL), hereby issues updated operational guidelines for emergency drawdown.

In the event of a structural supply disruption exceeding 20% of India's daily crude imports (approx. 0.9 mbpd) or a blockade of the Strait of Hormuz:
1.  **Authorized Release:** The Ministry may authorize the phased release of up to 45 million barrels of stored crude across Mangaluru, Padur, and Visakhapatnam caverns.
2.  **Refinery Allocation:** Drawdown crude shall be allocated based on refinery-specific processable grades (grade matching) and proximity pipelines, with priority given to public sector undertakings (PSUs) and refiners feeding critical domestic fuel markets.
3.  **Replenishment Thresholds:** Refilling operations must be initiated within 90 days of crisis stabilization. The target replenishment window is triggered when the Indian Crude Basket falls below $78/barrel, or when domestic market stability is restored.

By Order,
Joint Secretary (International Cooperation & Reserves), MoPNG
