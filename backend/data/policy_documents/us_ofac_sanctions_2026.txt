US Department of the Treasury
Office of Foreign Assets Control (OFAC)
Action: Executive Order 14120 — Imposing Sanctions with Respect to Specified Energy Corridors
Date: May 2, 2026

WASHINGTON — Today, the Department of the Treasury’s Office of Foreign Assets Control (OFAC) is expanding its sanctions registry targeting third-party entities engaged in the transport of sanctioned Iranian oil. 

Specifically, OFAC is designating 18 oil tankers and 4 international shipping registries that have facilitated trade in violation of existing price caps and secondary sanctions. These tankers are prohibited from berthing at ports of US allies, and their maritime insurance coverage under Western clubs is declared void.

Implications for Maritime Traffic & Indian Refineries:
This regulatory policy action directly affects the grey-market fleet transporting crude through the Persian Gulf and Strait of Hormuz. Refiners in the Asia-Pacific region, including Indian operators relying on secondary trade routes, face immediate shipping insurance cancellations and potential port docking issues for affected VLCCs. Expected delays at Middle East loading terminals are projected to increase by 4-7 days due to compliance checks.
