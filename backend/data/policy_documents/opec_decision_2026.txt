OPEC+ Press Release & Policy Directive
Date: April 14, 2026
Subject: OPEC+ Output Adjustments and Quota Extensions

VIENNA — The OPEC and non-OPEC Ministerial Meeting (OPEC+) has resolved to extend the voluntary crude oil production cuts of 1.65 million barrels per day (mbpd) through the end of the fourth quarter of 2026. This decision aims to support market stability amid rising non-OPEC supply and macro-economic headwinds. 

Additionally, Saudi Arabia and the Russian Federation announced they will carry over their extra voluntary production cuts of 1.0 mbpd and 471,000 bpd respectively, originally scheduled to expire in June, until December 31, 2026. 

Implications for India:
The extension of supply cuts is expected to maintain Brent crude price support, with prices forecasted to hover in the $80-$92 range under normal conditions. This reduces spot market availability of Saudi Arab Light and Russian Urals grades, increasing the cost of emergency spot purchases for Indian refiners. Strategic reserves should prepare for prolonged elevated pricing profiles.
