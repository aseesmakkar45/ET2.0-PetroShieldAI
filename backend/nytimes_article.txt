Strait of Hormuz Tanker Traffic Erodes Further as Oil Prices Rise.
Tensions in the Middle East have escalated following new maritime strikes near the Strait of Hormuz, a critical chokepoint for global energy transit. 
Tanker traffic through the strait has slowed by 15% as insurance premiums surge. Analysts warn that shipping disruptions could last for at least 30 days.
India, which relies heavily on crude imports passing through the Persian Gulf, is monitoring the situation. Refineries are preparing for potential delays in shipments from Saudi Arabia and Iraq, which make up a major portion of India's import mix.
Brent crude prices rose 4.5% on the news, settling at $84.20 per barrel.
